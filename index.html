OneMSU: Campus Mobile Consolidation

MSU students were juggling 5+ separate campus apps just to check dining hours, find parking, and access safety features. Through interviews with 8 students, I found that 68% abandoned tasks because they couldn't figure out which app had what they needed. I redesigned the information architecture around how students actually think about their tasks‚Äînot how IT departments organize systems‚Äîand made safety features accessible from any screen instead of buried in a separate app.

About Me

I'm a UX Researcher at the University of Michigan, and I'm fascinated by why people struggle with interfaces and how to fix them. I run usability tests, watch people interact with designs, and redesign based on what actually works‚Äînot what looks good in theory.

Right now I'm focused on physical interaction design‚Äîtouchscreens, mobile apps, anything people use with their hands in real-world contexts. I'm particularly interested in how context changes everything: a button that works at a desk might be impossible to hit while walking or wearing gloves.
